01 The ask, in your words

“Basically looking for every movie financing company in the last 10 years that has made horror and sci-fi movies in both America, Europe and Canada.” — The brief this deliverable is built to answer

One clarification is built into the design: you asked for companies that made these films — what you actually want is the ones that put up the money. A “producer” credit is often creative, not financial. So the engine classifies money vs. craft and reports only entities it can evidence as financiers, each with a confidence level.

03 Scope, precisely

Genre

Horror and science-fiction, including genre-blend titles that lean into either. Boundary confirmed with you before the run.

Territory

United States, Europe, and Canada — financiers operating in or co-producing across these markets.

Window

Financing activity in the last 10 years, dated by the deal — not the film’s release year.

Budget band

The ≤ $10M indie band first. Widening to all budget levels is a funded add-on, not a re-build.

Entity types

Companies & funds, sales agents / MG-providing distributors, tax-credit & soft-money bodies, and individual financiers where public.

Financed, not filmed

Only entities evidenced as putting up money — equity, MG advances, gap loans, soft money — not creative credits.

Genre note worth knowing: horror and sci-fi money is unusually sales-agent and MG-driven. Including those entity types isn’t padding — for this genre it’s where a lot of the real money sits.

04 What this can — and can’t — promise

✓ What we deliver

  • Every financing entity discoverable from public record — filings, trade deal announcements, festival and market data, funds’ own mandates.
  • Each one correctly qualified (money vs. craft) and ranked by genre-specific warm signal.
  • Provenance and a confidence level on every figure and every classification.
  • A living database that refreshes as new deals are announced.

✕ What we won’t claim

  • That it is “every” financier, full stop. A large share of sub-$10M genre money is private individuals, family offices, and single-film SPVs that leave no public trace.
  • A budget we can’t source — “unknown” is reported honestly, never invented.
  • A contact that isn’t verified — no pattern-guessed emails.

This honesty is the point of the tool. It delivers comprehensive and evidenced, not a false “complete.” The value is a short, correct, reachable list — the kind you can actually act on.
